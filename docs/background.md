## Phase 2: The 7-Day Method

Work on your chosen passage every single day. By the end of the week, you should be able to recite it fully from memory.

### Five Best Practices

1. **Read it Aloud:** Do this four times in a row, every single day.
2. **Write it by Hand:** Physical writing activates motor neurons. Use your handwritten copy for daily practice.
3. **Visualize:** Close your eyes and connect an image to the words. Imagine yourself inside the "story" of the verse.
4. **Recite (Active Recall):** Push your brain to produce the words from memory rather than just reading them.
5. **Externalize:** Recite the passage to another person.

### Science-Backed Tips

* **Study & Paraphrase:** You remember what you understand. Put the verse into your own words to deepen comprehension.
* **Teach it:** Explain the meaning of the passage to someone else.
* **The Sleep Trick:** Review your verse right before bed. Your brain "locks in" memories during sleep.
* **Consistency:** Set a specific time each day to avoid skipping.

---

## Phase 3: Long-Term Retention

Memorization is a temporary spark; retention is a lifelong flame. To keep the Scripture in your heart, follow this **Spaced Repetition** schedule:

* **Daily:** Review once a day for one month.
* **Weekly:** Review once a week for one year.
* **Monthly:** Review once a month for the rest of your life.

> **Pro Tip:** Use a system to stay organized. Whether it’s 3x5 index cards in a box, a recurring calendar appointment, or a dedicated memory app, choose the one you are most likely to stick with.
